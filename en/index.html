01 The thesis

Great software businesses compound. We invest where that demand is most durable.

Enterprise software earns its place when an organisation cannot easily run without it — when it is embedded in how the business operates, paid for year after year, and defended in weak years as readily as strong ones. We look for that durability: recurring revenue, high retention and a clear reason the customer stays. We hold particular conviction in one corner of the market — the software that regulated industries depend on — because the obligation behind that demand only widens, and rarely lapses.

01
Durable, recurring revenue

We favour businesses where the customer renews because the software is woven into daily operations — not because switching is merely inconvenient. Retention is the clearest evidence that the product is doing real work.

02
Defensible positions

A workflow others depend on, a data set that compounds with use, an integration that is costly to unwind. We underwrite the moat before the multiple, and pass when the advantage is only a head start.

03
Non-discretionary spend

Spend that keeps the business running — or that satisfies a legal obligation — sits below the line where cost-cutting operates, closer to audit fees than to the marketing stack. Demand is structural rather than cyclical.

04
Particular conviction: regulated-industry software

Where software answers a supervised, mandated obligation, the demand is among the most durable we underwrite. Regimes such as DORA and the EU AI Act add permanent, auditable work — one focus area within a broader software mandate, not the whole of it.

How we work

A repeatable method, applied the same way to every opportunity — discipline we can describe before any outcome is in.

01
Underwrite the demand

Before any assumption, we test that the demand is durable and recurring — structural, not cyclical. If the customer can walk away without cost, we pass.

02
The first hundred days

We agree the few decisions that compound — pricing, the product roadmap, the key hires — and set the reporting and board cadence that runs from day one.

04 Responsible investing

Good governance is the standard we hold ourselves to.

Much of the software we back exists to make organisations more accountable, more secure and better governed — which sets our standard for ourselves. We hold the firm and its portfolio to that standard from diligence onward, and the platforms we back raise the baseline of governance, evidence and resilience across the organisations that run them.

In diligence

Governance, data protection and security factors are assessed in every decision — integrated, not bolted on after.

In ownership

We expect strong security, privacy and board practice from day one — and we help build it where it's missing.

In impact

Every deployment makes downstream organisations more compliant and more resilient.

07 For prospective investors

Questions, answered.

What is the track record?

Northstone is an emerging manager. We have made one inaugural investment. We have no realised track record to present, and we will not imply one — our case rests on the thesis, the method and the structure set out on this page.

How many portfolio companies are there?

One today — an inaugural software investment. Details and further investments will be announced as the portfolio is assembled.

How can I invest alongside Northstone?

Two ways, both open to qualified investors. The Northstone Bond offers a defined-term, fixed-return exposure to our software thesis. An equity partnership makes you a co-owner of the holdings, sharing directly in the upside. Each is governed solely by its own formal subscription materials.

Who can invest?

Both routes are offered only to qualified investors, including institutions, family offices and eligible private investors, and access is subject to the eligibility rules of each investor's jurisdiction. Participation is governed solely by the relevant instrument's formal subscription materials.

What do you invest in?

Software companies with durable, recurring revenue and defensible positions, across Europe and North America — with particular conviction in the software that regulated industries depend on, where demand is structural rather than cyclical.

What is the typical hold period?

We are long-term owners, with holds typically running five to seven years. Buy-and-build platforms that consolidate complementary products may be held over a longer horizon, consistent with the terms set out in the materials.

Where are you based?

Northstone Invest is headquartered in Zurich, Switzerland. Our instruments are structured under Swiss law with independent administration, external audit and legal counsel. Full structure and terms are detailed in the materials available on request.

How do I request the materials?

Complete the form on our investor relations page and we will follow up to confirm eligibility and arrange an introductory call. Materials are shared only with qualified investors under applicable law.
