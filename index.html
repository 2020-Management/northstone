01 The thesis

Regulation only compounds. The software that manages it is becoming indispensable.

Every regulated organisation now carries a permanent, growing obligation to govern its data, prove its controls and quantify its risk. That work is moving off spreadsheets and onto dedicated platforms — and the budgets behind it are mandated, recurring and defended through every cycle. We invest where that demand is most durable.

01
Regulatory expansion

DORA, NIS2, the EU AI Act and SEC cyber rules keep widening the surface area of obligation — each regime adds permanent, auditable work.

02
From spreadsheets to systems

Compliance is migrating off email and spreadsheets onto purpose-built software. Exposure can no longer be managed by hand.

03
Non-discretionary spend

GRC budgets are mandated and recurring. Demand is structural, not cyclical — and resilient in downturns.

04
A fragmented market

Hundreds of point tools serve overlapping needs. We see a clear path to consolidate them into durable platforms.

04 Responsible investing

Good governance is the business we're in.

GRC software exists to make organisations more accountable, more secure and better governed. We hold ourselves to the standard our companies sell — and the platforms we back raise the floor for governance across every organisation that runs them.

In diligence

Governance, data protection and security factors are assessed in every decision — integrated, not bolted on after.

In ownership

We expect strong security, privacy and board practice from day one — and we help build it where it's missing.

In impact

Every deployment makes thousands of downstream organisations more compliant and more resilient.

07 For prospective investors

Questions, answered.

Who can invest in Fund I?+

Fund I is open to qualified institutional, family-office and private investors, subject to eligibility and the regulations of your jurisdiction.

What does the fund invest in?+

Control and growth equity in governance, risk and compliance (GRC) software companies across Europe and North America.

What is the typical hold period?+

We are long-term owners. Holds typically run five to seven years, with flexibility for buy-and-build platforms that compound over a longer horizon.
