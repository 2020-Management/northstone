The largest enterprises solved their GRC problem years ago, with heavyweight suites and the teams to run them. The smallest firms can still get by on spreadsheets and judgement. Between them sits the mid-market — large enough to carry real regulatory obligations, too lean to staff around them, and poorly served by software built for either extreme. That gap is where GRC software wins.

## Outgrowing the spreadsheet, priced out of the suite

A mid-market firm hits a wall the same way every time. The obligations multiply, the spreadsheets sprawl, an audit finding lands — and the enterprise suite that would fix it is too expensive, too slow to deploy and too heavy to operate. They need something that delivers most of the value at a fraction of the weight.

> The mid-market does not want less capability. It wants capability it can actually deploy.

## Why this is the durable segment

Mid-market buyers convert faster, deploy faster and — once a system becomes their record of compliance — stay. They lack the in-house teams to switch casually, which makes retention strong, and they grow into more modules as their obligations grow. The economics of serving them well are excellent.

- A large, underserved population of regulated firms.
- Shorter sales cycles and faster time-to-value than enterprise.
- Expansion as obligations and headcount grow.

We back the companies that meet this segment on its terms: quick to stand up, priced for the mid-market, and modular enough to grow with the customer. That is where the most attractive GRC software businesses are being built.
